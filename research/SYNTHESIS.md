# Redesign Synthesis: Key Insights for surdu.de

Synthesis of two research documents:
- [01-personal-website-inspiration.md](01-personal-website-inspiration.md) (10 sites: Cohen, Balfour, Doshi, Rachitsky, Hoover, Traynor, Rekhi, Dodds, Willison, Biddle)
- [02-builder-and-founder-sites.md](02-builder-and-founder-sites.md) (10 sites: Robinson, Rauch, Kahl, Sivers, Swyx, Yongfook, Levels, Pan, Postma, Shapiro)

Total: 20 reference sites, with ~zero overlap between the two docs, giving complementary perspectives. Doc 1 leans toward established product operators and advisors. Doc 2 leans toward bootstrapped/indie founders and technical builders.

---

## The Core Diagnosis (Both Docs Agree)

The current surdu.de reads like a **LinkedIn profile in HTML**: title, timeline, contact button. It proves competence but does not invite collaboration, communicate personality, or answer "why reach out to *this* person, for *what*."

> Move from **"here is my background"** to **"here is why founders trust me with hard product and technical work."**

---

## The Five Questions a Visitor Should Answer in 10 Seconds

Distilled from the recurring patterns across all 20 sites:

1. **Who do you help?** (founders, early-stage teams, technical co-founders)
2. **What do you help with?** (product strategy, shipping, tech-product translation)
3. **Why should they trust you?** (proof, specificity, visible track record)
4. **How do you think?** (essays, case studies, public work, "now" page)
5. **How do they reach you?** (one specific CTA, not a generic "Contact Me")

---

## High-Confidence Patterns (Mentioned in Both Docs)

These appear independently in both research docs, so treat them as the strongest signals.

### 1. First-person, value-led hero (replaces the title hero)
Both docs agree the current "Senior Product Manager with 10+ years..." is employment-coded. Replace with one sentence about *who you help and what changes for them*.
- Doc 1 references: Cohen, Doshi, Rekhi.
- Doc 2 references: Kahl ("I empower founders & creators..."), Swyx ("Writer, Founder, Devtools Startup Advisor..."), Robinson.

### 2. Visible thinking layer (essays, notes, case studies)
A small but real archive that shows judgment, not polish. 3-6 pieces is enough to start.
- Doc 1: Cohen, Traynor, Willison, Rekhi.
- Doc 2: Shapiro, Swyx, Pan (case studies specifically).

### 3. Selected work / projects / "what I'm building"
A short list of concrete artifacts. For a PM with engineering history, this is the missing layer that turns a CV into a builder profile.
- Doc 1: Hoover's projects section, Rekhi's archive.
- Doc 2: Levels' /projects, Yongfook, Pan's case studies, Postma's open building.

### 4. Personality / humanizing layer
Sport, music, family, taste. Doc 1 frames this as "tying discipline back to how you work." Doc 2 frames it as "founders hire *people*."
- Doc 1: Dodds (warmth), the Ironman line you already have.
- Doc 2: Robinson (music, family), Sivers (philosophy), Postma ("how I work").

### 5. One specific collaboration CTA
Replace "Contact Me" with intent. "Tell me what you're building" / "Open to advising, co-building, consulting" / "Let's work together."
- Doc 1: Doshi's "Let's Work Together."
- Doc 2: explicit table recommendation for surdu.de.

## Patterns Unique to Each Doc (Worth Considering)

**Only in Doc 1 (operator/advisor lens):**
- **Proof strip under hero** with concrete numbers (years, scale, P&L). Borrowed from Cohen/Balfour/Rekhi.
- **"Ways I can help" section** with 2-3 named offerings. Doshi is the model.
- **Light testimonial layer** (even 2 short quotes). Biddle is the model.
- **Rollout sequenced as messaging > proof > examples > polish.** Don't polish the timeline before fixing positioning.

**Only in Doc 2 (builder/indie lens):**
- **`/now` page.** Sivers' invention; low effort, high trust. Shows current focus and what you want to attract.
- **Narrative About page** tracing Game Dev to Team Lead to PM as a learning journey, not a job list. Rauch is the model.
- **"README about me" format** (treating yourself as an open-source project). Swyx, technical-audience friendly.
- **Build/ship in public signal** with active links to projects, even small ones. Levels, Yongfook, Postma.
- **Hand-coded aesthetic as a signal of taste.** Sivers, Levels. Already partially aligned with current static-HTML setup.

## Cross-Doc Consensus on the Tone Target

| Spectrum | Avoid | Aim for |
|---|---|---|
| Polish | Templated / placeholder text (Biddle pitfall) | Clean but personal |
| Voice | Corporate / "passionate about startups" | Specific, candid, first-person |
| Authority | Cool/distant operator (Balfour pitfall) | Warm operator (Hoover, Robinson) |
| Content density | Blog-forward (Willison pitfall) | Curated thinking + visible building |
| Branding | Creator-brand / advisor platform | Peer-like builder, founder-friendly |

The sweet spot the redesign should target sits roughly at **Hoover + Rekhi + Kahl**: credible operator, current builder, one-line clear positioning.

## Concrete Section List for the Redesign

A merged proposal pulling the strongest moves from both docs:

1. **Hero** — first-person value statement + face + one specific CTA. (Both docs)
2. **Proof strip** — 3-4 concrete numbers/credentials in one line. (Doc 1)
3. **Selected work / "Things I've built"** — 3 short snapshots: problem, role, outcome. (Both docs)
4. **Ways I can help** — 2-3 named collaboration modes. (Doc 1)
5. **Now / current focus** — what you're building or exploring this quarter. (Doc 2)
6. **Thinking** — 3-6 short essays, notes, or case studies. (Both docs)
7. **About / origin story** — narrative arc, not a timeline. (Doc 2; replaces current timeline)
8. **Human layer** — Ironman, AI, taste, tied to working style. (Both docs)
9. **Light social proof** — 1-2 quotes or named outcomes. (Doc 1)
10. **Footer** — auto-year, real contact path, honest "currently at InnoGames" line. (Doc 1 small-details note)

---

## Recommended Sequencing

Doc 1's rollout order is the right one. Don't redesign visuals first.

1. **Positioning** — rewrite hero, subhead, CTA. (1 week)
2. **Proof + freshness** — proof strip, fix footer year, refresh dates. (1 week)
3. **Evidence** — 3 work snapshots, "ways I can help." (1-2 weeks)
4. **Thinking** — first 2-3 essays or notes; set up `/now`. (2 weeks)
5. **Polish** — narrative About, human layer, light testimonials. (1 week)
6. **Iterate** — review inbound replies, tighten copy.

---

## Single Sharpest Insight

If only one change ships: **rewrite the hero into one first-person sentence that names who you help and what changes, and pair it with a CTA that names the collaboration mode.** Both docs converge on this as the highest-leverage move. Everything else is amplification.

Example direction (not final copy):
> "I'm Andrei. I help founders turn product ideas into shipped, scalable software, drawing on 13 years of going from game developer to senior PM at InnoGames. Tell me what you're building."
